Minor Surgery

Minor surgery, as offered by the clinic, falls under the general category of surgical care, which focuses on diagnosing and treating diseases, injuries, and deformities through operative techniques.

Examples of Minor Surgical Procedures

  • Wound suturing
  • Minimally Invasive Surgery
  • Laparoscopic procedures
  • Endoscopic procedures.
  • Biopsy (Punch, Shave, or Incisional)
  • Foreign Body Removal
Pre-operative: Comprehensive evaluations and diagnostic tests. Post-operative: Care includes pain management, wound care, and monitoring for any signs of infection or complications to promote faster healing.

General Definition of Minor Surgery

Minor surgery is characterized by its low risk of complications, being minimally invasive (avoiding the opening of major body cavities), reliance on local anesthesia or minimal sedation, and being performed on an outpatient basis (allowing the patient to return home the same day), resulting in a quick recovery and reduced scarring.
